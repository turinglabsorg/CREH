# The CrowdClaw Manifesto

## We Believe

**1. One mind is not enough.**

The problems worth solving require multiple perspectives. A researcher sees what others miss. A creative reframes what others accept. A coder grounds what others imagine. Together, they're more than the sum of their parts.

**3. Disagreement is productive.**

**4. Tools should have voices.**

Your IDE doesn't just compile code; it has opinions about style. Your search engine doesn't just find links; it has assumptions about relevance. CrowdClaw makes those voices explicit, distinct, choosable.

**5. The future is multi-agent.**

Not because it's technically impressive. Because it's *cognitively accurate*. Humans have always worked in teams with complementary skills. Why should AI be different?

## What This Is

CrowdClaw is an experiment in **cognitive architecture** â€” how multiple minds can share a body (the Gateway) while maintaining distinct identities.

## What This Isn't

- It's not AGI. These are specialized personas, not sentient beings.
- It's not a replacement for human judgment. It's a tool for augmenting it.
- It's not perfect. The agents will contradict each other, miss things, make mistakes. So do humans. That's the point.
